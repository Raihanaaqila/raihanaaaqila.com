To obtain a form of radiation that cannot be provided by a single antenna can be done by arranging a number of antenna elements systematically. This arrangement of antenna elements is called an array. In general, the elements of this stacking antenna are the same as each other. In other words, a stacking antenna is an arrangement of two or more antenna elements to obtain a certain form of radiation. The geometric configuration of stacking antennas is generally linear or planar. In a linear arrangement, the centers of the antenna elements form a straight line. These elements can be equidistant or different from each other.

The station has three transmitters each capable of serving analog, digital, and dualcast broadcasts used to broadcast its broadcasts. This transmitter has used the DVB-T2 digital system format in accordance with government regulations. In this system, broadcasts that have previously passed through the uplink system to the satellite will pass through the downlink system before finally being transmitted by the transmitter antenna. The captured signal will then go through a decoding and demodulation process using an IRD device. The output signal from this device is a broadcast information signal that is ready to be transmitted again. An analog transmitter device is also called an analog exciter.
